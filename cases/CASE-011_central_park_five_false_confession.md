## Decision Context at Time T

Investigators believe a brutal, high-profile assault must be solved quickly. Several juveniles are in custody for related disorder and assault activity in the park, but the evidentiary link to the rape is weak and unclear. Under intense public pressure, investigators consider extended accusatory interrogation, deceptive pressure, and confession-first case construction to secure a prosecution.

## Available Information at Time T (Ex-Ante Only)

- the crime is severe, politically explosive, and highly publicized
- several juveniles are already in custody for other misconduct in the area
- direct evidence linking the juveniles to the rape is weak, incomplete, or inconsistent
- prolonged interrogation and deception may produce statements that look probative even when false
- juveniles are especially vulnerable to compliance, confusion, exhaustion, and authority pressure
- once confessions are obtained, courts, prosecutors, and juries may overweight them relative to conflicting evidence

## Dominant Consensus Position at Time T

Given the seriousness of the crime and the belief that the youths were involved somehow, aggressive interrogation is justified to secure the truth and protect the public.

## Dissenting Views at Time T

Some legal and ethical observers argue that confession-first policing under evidentiary weakness creates acute false-confession risk, especially with juveniles, and can contaminate the entire case before reliable corroboration exists.

## Institutional Incentives and Roles

- police face extreme pressure to solve a notorious violent crime quickly
- political and media pressure reward visible progress and apparent closure
- prosecutors may treat confessions as anchor evidence even when provenance is tainted
- judges and juries may give confession evidence disproportionate weight
- juveniles depend heavily on procedural safeguards that may erode under pressure

## Eventual Downstream Outcomes (Retrospective Only)

The case later became a paradigmatic wrongful-conviction and false-confession scandal, with the original confessions discredited and the convictions vacated after stronger evidence pointed elsewhere.

## Retrospective Scholarly / Narrative Assessment

Treated as a canonical criminal-justice procedural-hazard case in which pressure, vulnerability, and confession contamination overran evidentiary discipline.

## Falsifiers / Disconfirming Conditions

- evidence that interrogation conditions were noncoercive, fully documented, and procedurally robust
- evidence that the statements were independently corroborated by strong physical evidence known before contamination
- evidence that investigators did not use deceptive or pressure-amplifying tactics on vulnerable suspects
