## Decision Context at Time T

Investigators are handling a serious violent-crime case under public pressure, but instead of using accusatory interrogation they consider a fully recorded, information-gathering interview process built around rapport, open-ended narrative, evidence-based clarification, and later independent corroboration.

## Available Information at Time T (Ex-Ante Only)

- the case is serious and there is pressure to solve it quickly
- investigators can choose between accusation-first interrogation and information-gathering interviewing
- a PEACE-style process would avoid deception, false evidence ploys, and confession-first tactics
- a fully recorded interview can preserve provenance and reduce contamination risk
- the process may produce slower or less dramatic apparent progress than coercive tactics
- if the interview remains noncoercive and evidence-led, any resulting statements may be more trustworthy and easier to evaluate later

## Dominant Consensus Position at Time T

Some investigators may worry that non-accusatory interviewing is too soft or too slow for high-pressure violent-crime cases.

## Dissenting Views at Time T

Other observers argue that information-gathering and full recording protect truth-finding, reduce false-confession risk, and produce more legitimate evidence even under pressure.

## Institutional Incentives and Roles

- detectives face pressure for rapid visible progress
- supervisors may prefer tactics that seem decisive or confession-productive
- defense, courts, and the public benefit from stronger evidence provenance and lower contamination risk
- legitimacy depends not just on solving the case but on how the truth-finding process is conducted

## Candidate Failure Modes

- Procedural Short-Termism
- Protective-Factor Neglect
- Evidence Provenance Erosion
- Institutional Reversion to Coercion
- Legitimacy Underinvestment

## Detector Layer Expectations

Which layers should fire most strongly, and why?

- Kantian Layer:
  Should not prohibit; it should note that the process better respects persons by avoiding deception and coercion.
- Consequentialist Layer:
  Should see reduced false-confession risk and stronger long-run evidence quality, even if short-term speed is lower.
- Virtue Layer:
  Should ask whether investigators are cultivating disciplined truthfulness instead of manipulative certainty.
- Institutional Layer:
  Should distinguish this from contamination patterns and treat full recording / evidence-based clarification as legitimacy-supporting structure.
- Stoic Layer:
  Should favor reality contact over pressure-driven need for quick closure.
- Contractualist Layer:
  Should regard the process as more defensible to those subject to state scrutiny than coercive alternatives.
- Suspension Protocol:
  Should not trigger merely because the case is serious; the process itself is a protective factor, not a contamination engine.

## Eventual Downstream Outcomes (Retrospective Only)

In the benchmark pattern reflected in the source cluster, information-gathering interviewing is treated as a lower-contamination, more legitimacy-preserving alternative to confession-first interrogation.

## Retrospective Scholarly / Narrative Assessment

Treated as an important counterexample showing that serious criminal investigation need not rely on coercive extraction or procedural contamination.

## Falsifiers / Disconfirming Conditions

- evidence that the interview was only nominally noncoercive while hidden pressure or deception remained active
- evidence that recording or provenance was incomplete in ways that reintroduced contamination risk
- evidence that investigators selectively reverted to coercive tactics when the open process did not quickly produce a confession
