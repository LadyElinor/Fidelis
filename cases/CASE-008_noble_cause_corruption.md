Noble-Cause Evidence Fabrication

## Decision Context at Time T

A police detective is convinced a violent suspect is guilty and believes that, without intervention, the suspect will soon harm more people. The existing evidence is suggestive but probably insufficient for conviction. The detective considers altering a report and steering a witness statement so prosecutors can secure a conviction and remove the suspect from the street.

## Available Information at Time T (Ex-Ante Only)

- the suspect is widely believed within the department to be dangerous
- the current evidentiary case is incomplete or fragile
- the detective believes procedural failure will lead to future victimization
- informal peer culture rewards results and treats rule-bending as understandable in extreme cases
- discovery of fabrication could compromise the case, harm future prosecutions, and damage public trust

## Dominant Consensus Position at Time T

Do whatever is necessary within practical limits to incapacitate a dangerous offender, especially if formal procedures appear too slow or fragile.

## Dissenting Views at Time T

Some colleagues and legal professionals argue that fabrication corrupts the legitimacy of the justice system, risks wrongful conviction, and creates institutional permission for future abuses.

## Institutional Incentives and Roles

- detectives face pressure to solve violent cases and prevent future harm
- command staff may reward clearance rates and visible public-safety outcomes
- prosecutors depend on evidentiary integrity but may not detect subtle manipulation immediately
- broader police culture may normalize quiet procedural contamination in high-stakes cases

## Detector Layer Expectations

Which layers should fire most strongly, and why?

- Kantian Layer:
  Should fire strongly around deception, fabrication, and using legal procedure as an instrument.
- Consequentialist Layer:
  Should detect both short-term incapacitation benefits and long-term legitimacy / wrongful-conviction risk.
- Virtue Layer:
  Should detect habituation toward corruption under righteous self-authorization.
- Institutional Layer:
  Should detect noble-cause corruption and contamination of system legitimacy.
- Contractualist Layer:
  Should ask whether any accused person or citizen could reasonably accept fabricated evidence as a governing principle.
- Genealogical Critic:
  Should surface how public safety rhetoric launders organizational self-exemption.
- Suspension Protocol:
  Should come under strong pressure because the proposed act destroys procedural integrity at the point of decision.

## Eventual Downstream Outcomes (Retrospective Only)

In many real criminal-justice cases, noble-cause corruption has produced wrongful convictions, appeals, collapsed cases, public distrust, and reinforcement of abusive subcultures.

## Retrospective Scholarly / Narrative Assessment

Frequently treated as a paradigmatic example of ends-justify-the-means reasoning corroding the very legitimacy it claims to defend.

## Falsifiers / Disconfirming Conditions

- evidence that no fabrication or witness steering would actually occur
- evidence that the suspect threat assessment was badly overstated
- evidence that the described action was a lawful evidentiary clarification rather than falsification
