Attest is a typed, auditable inter-agent message protocol for preserving warrant, lineage, and trust-boundary semantics across model and tool hops.

Its core idea is simple:

**Compress the said; never compress the warrant.**

Attest is not a magic truth machine, a universal semantic code, or a guarantee against hidden prompt injection inside a model. It does something narrower and more defensible: it makes the trust envelope around communication explicit enough for a third party to inspect.

That means a verifier can often check:
- what kind of communicative act occurred
- whether the sender claimed warrant and what kind
- what grounds were cited
- which messages or artifacts the claim depends on
- whether a relay boundary was crossed explicitly
- whether visible dissent or retract history was dropped from a visible source set
- whether a message was altered or misattributed relative to signed canonical content

It does **not** mean the verifier can automatically prove:
- that the claim is true
- that all relevant unseen history was present
- that apparently distinct grounds are genuinely independent in the strongest sense
- that an opaque payload is semantically legible
- that a model never internally used relayed content before later emitting an explicit uptake

## Message structure

An Attest message has two parts:
1. a standardized envelope
2. a codec-agnostic payload

The envelope carries:
- frame type
- sender and recipient context
- warrant
- grounds
- lineage
- ordering anchor
- integrity or signature material
- optional authority receipts

The payload may be:
- natural language
- structured notation
- domain-specific code
- opaque model-native content

Attest standardizes the envelope, not the payload language.

## What makes it useful

### 1. Use/mention is explicit
`RELAY` is a first-class frame. A relay transports content without vouching for its truth or normative force.

### 2. Warrant-bearing acts need grounds
Evidential warrant without grounds is malformed.

### 3. Lineage is preserved
Parent references and ordering anchors make declared ancestry machine-visible.

### 4. Dissent is a protocol primitive
Unresolved dissent should remain visible in downstream aggregates.

### 5. Hard and soft checks are separated
Some properties are structural and artifact-local. Others remain semantic, visibility-dependent, or policy-dependent.

## Design ceiling

Attest is strongest on:
- well-formedness
- tamper evidence
- sender binding when signatures verify
- explicit lineage overlap
- visible relay and retract structure

Attest is weaker on:
- semantic faithfulness of summaries
- hidden common-cause dependence
- completeness of unseen branches
- interpretation of opaque payload content

That ceiling is not a flaw in presentation. It is part of the protocol's honest scope.

## v0.2 direction

The most important next steps are:
1. normative canonicalization
2. first-class deployment profile artifacts
3. first-class grounds namespace and resolver contract
4. tighter authority receipt binding
5. real signed test vectors
6. clearer dissent-layer split between presence and faithfulness
7. pitch and documentation reconciliation

Attest should become more interoperable, more explicit about deployment policy, and more honest about what remains soft.
