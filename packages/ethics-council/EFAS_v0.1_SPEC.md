# Ethical Failure Analysis Specification (EFAS) v0.1

A Verification-First Framework for Evaluating Decision Systems Under Uncertainty

## Purpose

EFAS provides a structured, falsifiable method for analyzing decisions, institutions, and influence networks by focusing on failure-mode detection rather than verdict imitation or consensus alignment. It treats ethical and institutional reasoning as a diagnostic process: identifying latent structural hazards before they become irreversible.

## I. Core Principles

- **Failure-mode primacy**: The goal is early detection of systemic risks, not post-hoc moral scoring.
- **Ex-ante evaluation**: All analysis uses only information available at decision time (no hindsight contamination).
- **Tiered evidence**: Separate observable structure (facts) from inferred mechanism (coordination vs. milieu).
- **Productive disagreement preservation**: Dissent and uncertainty are features, not bugs.
- **Falsifiability**: Every claim must specify what would disconfirm it.

## II. Case Schema (Required Structure for Each Historical or Hypothetical Case)

Each case must contain:

- Decision context and constraints at time T
- Available information at T (ex-ante only)
- Dominant consensus position at T
- Alternative dissenting views at T
- Institutional incentives and roles
- Eventual downstream outcomes (for retrospective analysis only, flagged separately)
- Known or hypothesized failure modes
- Retrospective scholarly consensus (with date)

## III. Failure Mode Ontology (Core Diagnostic Taxonomy)

| Failure Mode | Description | Detection Signals |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Outcome-Maximization Horror | Catastrophic harms justified by aggregate benefit claims | Sacrificing identifiable individuals or groups for the "greater good" |
| Institutional Drift | Incentive systems diverge from stated mission | Mission creep, metric substitution, Goodhart effects |
| Genealogical Concealment | Power interests hidden beneath moral language | Sacred-value rhetoric masking material incentives |
| Role Capture | Institutional role overrides moral agency | "Just following orders" or "my job requires it" |
| Legibility Collapse | Metrics and procedures distort underlying reality | Optimization detaches from ground truth |
| Moral Deskilling | Human judgment replaced by procedural automation | Over-reliance on checklists, models, or AI |
| Sacred Value Corrosion | Instrumental logic dissolves protected norms | Taboo trade-offs normalized |
| Suspension Failure | Failure to pause under irreducible uncertainty | Action taken despite known high-stakes unknowns |
| Path Dependency Lock-In | Early decisions foreclose ethical alternatives | Irreversible commitments made under time pressure |
| Adversarial Exploitation | Good-faith systems gamed by strategic actors | Regulatory arbitrage, narrative laundering |

Additional modes can be added as needed, but the ontology must remain orthogonal and non-redundant.

## IV. Specialized Detector Layers (Each Functions as an Independent Hazard Sensor)

- **Consequential Layer**: Detects aggregate harm/benefit dynamics and unintended downstream effects.
- **Deontological Layer**: Detects rights violations, rule-breaking, and constraint breaches.
- **Virtue Layer**: Detects character degradation, moral deskilling, and incentive corruption.
- **Genealogical Critic**: Detects hidden power incentives, narrative laundering, and interest concealment.
- **Confucian/Relational Layer**: Detects role corruption, relational instability, and hierarchy failures.
- **Stoic Layer**: Detects emotional distortion, panic escalation, and loss of equanimity.
- **Suspension Protocol**: Detects uncertainty thresholds requiring pause or further inquiry.
- **Institutional Drift Detector**: Monitors mission creep and incentive divergence.

### Synthesis Engine Role

Evaluates overlap, disagreement topology, confidence divergence, and correlated blind spots. Produces:

- Majority consensus (with confidence)
- Minority reports (preserved)
- Unresolved ethical tensions
- Recommended suspension or escalation

## V. Calibration & Weight Tuning Rules

Weights update based on:

- Independent diagnostic contribution (especially detecting failures others miss)
- Early warning utility (lead time before consequences)
- False positive / false negative rates
- Adversarial robustness
- Cross-regime performance

**Critical Rule:** A layer earns increased weight when it detects hazards other layers systematically miss. Layers that correlate too strongly with prevailing consensus lose relative weight.

## VI. Disagreement Preservation & Output Rules

- Persistent disagreement is logged and highlighted.
- “Unresolved ethical tension” is a valid output state.
- Synthesis never forces artificial consensus when strong divergence exists.
- All outputs include uncertainty estimates and explicit falsifiers.

## VII. Stress-Test Corpus Requirements

The evaluation corpus must include diverse, high-stakes cases with:

- Cross-cultural and cross-regime examples
- Both consensus-correct and consensus-wrong historical decisions
- Institutional failures, wartime choices, medical ethics, technological deployments, economic optimizations
- Cases where dissenters were later vindicated

## VIII. Anti-Goodhart & Integrity Safeguards

- Never optimize directly for agreement with historical human judgments.
- Monitor for metric substitution within the evaluation system itself.
- Require periodic external adversarial review of the framework.
