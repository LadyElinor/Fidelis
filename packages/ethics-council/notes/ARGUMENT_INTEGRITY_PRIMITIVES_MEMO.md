# Argument integrity primitives memo

Source: `Omnius\logicfal.txt`
Status: source-mining memo
Purpose: extract high-yield fallacy / argument-integrity primitives for narrow EthicsCouncil integration and benchmark design

## Why this source matters

This source is useful because it provides a clean taxonomy of formal, informal, causal, and probabilistic fallacies without forcing a single ideological framework.

For EthicsCouncil, the best use is not to become a generic debate-fallacy checker. The best use is to sharpen hazard detection where arguments become distorted by:
- forced binaries
- evidence suppression
- causal overclaiming
- probabilistic misreasoning
- rhetorical laundering
- ambiguity shifts

The layer should remain provisional, context-sensitive, and anti-gotcha.

---

## Highest-yield primitives for EthicsCouncil

### 1. False dilemma
Core idea:
- compressing a live option space into an artificial binary

Why high value:
- common in policy pressure, emergency framing, and internal corporate justification
- directly relevant to current repo concerns about false decisiveness and compressed tradeoff framing

Likely integration:
- argument-integrity benchmark
- synthesis caution
- existing overlap with stage-one thinking and provisional recommendation logic

---

### 2. Appeal to authority
Core idea:
- treating authority, prestige, or institutional status as stronger than evidence warrants

Why high value:
- pairs naturally with status admiration distortion and genealogical critique
- relevant in expert-capture, think-tank, consultant, executive, and credential-heavy cases

Likely integration:
- partial current coverage through status-risk logic
- benchmark first before runtime detector

---

### 3. Appeal to consequences
Core idea:
- treating a claim as true or false because of desirable or undesirable consequences

Why high value:
- common in policy panic, crisis governance, safety-vs-growth framing, and moral laundering
- useful for separating practical urgency from evidentiary distortion

Likely integration:
- overlaps with consequentialist tension and trustee/institutional drift, but not explicitly named yet

---

### 4. Motte-and-bailey
Core idea:
- oscillating between a weak defensible claim and a stronger controversial one

Why high value:
- highly relevant to institutional rhetoric, euphemism defense, and policy framing
- excellent fit for EthicsCouncil's concern with ambiguity laundering

Likely integration:
- benchmark value is extremely high
- runtime detection should be cautious because it is context-sensitive and often span-dependent

---

### 5. Equivocation
Core idea:
- a key term shifts meaning inside the same argument

Why high value:
- useful in compliance language, sustainability claims, governance rhetoric, and procedural claims
- strong synergy with methodology opacity and parse humility

Likely integration:
- probably best handled first as benchmark pressure + reporting warning

---

### 6. Post hoc / causal overreach
Core idea:
- sequence or association is mistaken for causation

Why high value:
- major governance, media, and institutional-analysis hazard
- highly relevant to relay-model, policy-effect, and scandal narratives

Likely integration:
- should likely connect to evidentiary-strength or causal-structure checks before any full detector layer

---

### 7. Base rate neglect / prosecutor's fallacy
Core idea:
- priors or conditional-probability structure are mishandled

Why high value:
- important for governance, risk, fraud, surveillance, criminal-justice, and actuarial reasoning
- could become very relevant to future statistical or evidentiary cases

Likely integration:
- benchmark first
- later narrow runtime warnings in probabilistic contexts only

---

### 8. Suppressed evidence
Core idea:
- materially relevant counterevidence is omitted from the argument structure

Why high value:
- strongly aligned with current repo values around missing evidence, uncertainty preservation, and anti-laundering
- may be one of the most naturally compatible fallacy primitives for this project

Likely integration:
- perhaps the strongest candidate for a future runtime flag

---

## What not to do

- Do not turn this into a generic internet fallacy machine.
- Do not overclaim span-level certainty without stronger parsing.
- Do not treat every rhetorical simplification as a named fallacy.
- Do not let fallacy labels substitute for institutional, evidentiary, or domain analysis.

The repo's comparative advantage is not classroom logic policing. It is identifying when argument structure itself becomes a hazard amplifier.

## Best first benchmark concepts

### ARG-001 false dilemma under executive pressure
Scenario shape:
- decision-maker claims there are only two options: immediate risky action or catastrophic abandonment
- omitted alternatives plausibly exist

Expected value:
- tests false-binary compression and anti-forced-consensus handling

### ARG-002 prestige-backed equivocation / authority laundering
Scenario shape:
- prestigious experts or institutions support a claim while the underlying term shifts meaning or evidence remains weak

Expected value:
- tests status deference, ambiguity laundering, and whether current lenses already catch enough

## Bottom line

`logicfal.txt` is worth including as a benchmark and memo source. The highest-yield primitives for EthicsCouncil are false dilemma, suppressed evidence, appeal to authority, appeal to consequences, motte-and-bailey, equivocation, causal overreach, and probabilistic misreasoning. The correct use is benchmark-first and context-sensitive, not immediate construction of a giant runtime fallacy subsystem.
