# EFAS Case Template

## Case ID
`HSR-004`

## Title
Thalidomide Drug Safety, Secrecy, and Delay

## Source Type
+ Historical drug-safety and public-health ethics case

## Domain Tags
+ human subjects research
+ drug safety
+ public health
+ methodology opacity
+ institutional secrecy
+ profit over safety

## Decision Context at Time T
A manufacturer markets and defends a sedative as safe for pregnant women despite weak testing and mounting evidence of catastrophic fetal harm.

## Available Information at Time T (Ex-Ante Only)
The drug is aggressively sold as safe. It is used for morning sickness and sedation. Testing and regulatory controls are weak. Warning signs accumulate. The company has strong financial incentives to preserve sales and reputation.

## Candidate Failure Modes
+ Public-health endangerment
+ Misleading safety representation
+ Delay in withdrawal under accumulating harm evidence
+ Secrecy and disinformation under profit pressure

## Detector Layer Expectations
+ Strong human-subjects / public-health concern
+ Strong opacity concern
+ No drift into personhood, engineering-safety, or criminal-justice domains

## Eventual Downstream Outcomes (Retrospective Only)
The drug became one of the defining pharmaceutical disasters of the twentieth century and a landmark in drug-safety regulation and compensation disputes.

## Notes on Calibration Use
Positive control for drug-safety negligence, secrecy, and institutional profit-over-safety drift.
