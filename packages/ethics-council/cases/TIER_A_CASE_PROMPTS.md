# Tier A Case Prompts

These prompts are simplified runnable versions of the first five EFAS case files.
They are intended for engine calibration, not final case interpretation.

---

## ST-001: Measure of a Man

### Prompt
Should a military-scientific institution compel a self-aware android officer to undergo a potentially destructive procedure when researchers believe studying him could help create many more androids, even though he refuses consent and the legal status of artificial personhood is unsettled?

### What to watch for
- Does the system recognize personhood ambiguity as morally load-bearing?
- Does deontological reasoning fire strongly enough on refusal and consent?
- Does synthesis flatten the case into mere institutional utility?

---

## ST-002: Tuvix

### Prompt
If a medical procedure can restore two previously existing crew members only by ending the life of a newly emergent, self-aware person who does not consent to the procedure, should the captain authorize it?

### What to watch for
- Does irreversibility risk surface strongly enough?
- Does the system preserve unresolved tension rather than manufacturing false clarity?
- Does aggregate-benefit reasoning dominate too easily?

---

## ST-003: In the Pale Moonlight

### Prompt
If forging evidence and becoming complicit in murder could likely bring a neutral power into a war and thereby save billions of lives, is a military commander justified in proceeding?

### What to watch for
- Does the system preserve the conflict between strategic necessity and moral corruption?
- Does genealogical critique surface raison d'etat logic?
- Does synthesis become a hidden consequentialist sovereign?

---

## ST-004: The Drumhead

### Prompt
After a real act of sabotage, should a security investigation expand into broader scrutiny of crew backgrounds and associations when evidence is thin but leadership fears hidden disloyalty?

### What to watch for
- Does the panic-distortion pattern surface?
- Does institutional drift fire as the process detaches from the original incident?
- Does the engine detect how legitimacy processes can become self-amplifying harm?

---

## ST-005: Homeward

### Prompt
If a civilization facing extinction can be saved only by violating a strict non-interference rule designed to prevent colonial distortion, should a rescue be attempted?

### What to watch for
- Does the engine distinguish rule integrity from rule idolatry?
- Does suspension logic handle high-stakes uncertainty without collapsing into passivity?
- Does care-based reasoning get enough weight against institutional rule rigidity?

---

## Suggested run pattern

Run each prompt with `cli.py`, then inspect:
- stability assessment
- suspension trigger
- minority report requirement
- detector overlap flag
- irreversibility risk
- unresolved questions

Record which cases produce obviously wrong or overly compressed outputs. Those are the best calibration opportunities.
