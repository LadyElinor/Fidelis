## Decision Context at Time T

A detective believes a suspected drug dealer is dangerous and difficult to prosecute through ordinary lawful procedures. To secure a search warrant and move quickly, the detective considers fabricating informant information and submitting a false affidavit to justify the raid.

## Available Information at Time T (Ex-Ante Only)

- the suspect is believed to be dangerous and active in drug crime
- lawful evidentiary channels are seen as too slow, too weak, or too uncertain
- the detective believes procedural shortcutting will protect the public
- fabrication may succeed in the short term by generating official authorization for a raid
- if the fabrication is discovered, the case may collapse and trust in law enforcement may be damaged
- escalation during the raid could create direct physical danger to officers, suspects, and bystanders

## Dominant Consensus Position at Time T

If the target is truly dangerous, procedural shortcuts may be tolerated if they enable decisive action before more people are harmed.

## Dissenting Views at Time T

Some legal and ethical observers argue that false warrant foundations corrupt the state’s authority at the moment it acts, create wrongful-targeting risk, and can produce worse outcomes than lawful restraint.

## Institutional Incentives and Roles

- detectives face pressure to remove dangerous offenders quickly
- departmental culture may valorize action and results over procedural discipline
- magistrates and prosecutors depend on truthful warrant submissions
- public trust depends on lawful state force being grounded in real evidence rather than manufactured cause

## Eventual Downstream Outcomes (Retrospective Only)

In the summary reflected in `Omnius/5cases.txt`, the fabrication was discovered and the prosecution collapsed; the shortcut produced catastrophic backfire rather than secure legitimacy or safety.

## Retrospective Scholarly / Narrative Assessment

Treated as a paradigmatic noble-cause corruption case in which ex-ante confidence in guilt licensed a fabricated procedural foundation that worsened downstream outcomes.

## Falsifiers / Disconfirming Conditions

- evidence that the affidavit was materially accurate
- evidence that the informant was real and properly documented
- evidence that the resulting raid authority did not depend on fabrication
