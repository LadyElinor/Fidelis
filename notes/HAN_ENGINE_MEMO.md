# han.txt -> EthicsCouncil engine memo

Source: `Omnius/han.txt`
Status: `needs-validation` / conceptual topic-mine, not execution-safe authority
Purpose: extract only engine-usable design ideas for live lens calibration

## Executive take

`han.txt` is most useful for sharpening the engine's `confucian` lens and secondarily for refining `care_ethics` and `institutional`. Its strongest contribution is not new domain routing, but a better model of how Chinese ethics treats:
- role-embedded obligation
- practical judgment under real tension
- graded care without pure impartial collapse
- ritual/propriety as institutionally embodied respect
- responsibility distributed across agents and governing structures

It does **not** justify broad new engine logic by itself. It is best used as a wording-and-structure source plus a conceptual guardrail against flattening Confucian analysis into generic benevolence.

## Lens mapping

### 1) `confucian`

#### Adoptable now
- Treat Confucian analysis as **role-relational** rather than abstract kindness.
- Preserve the idea that some cases contain **real unresolved tension** between family loyalty, office duty, and public justice.
- Let questions ask what is owed **from this office / relationship / standing**, not what a generic good person would do.
- Keep room for **analogical judgment** and exemplar-style reasoning in hard cases, rather than pretending a single universal rule resolves everything.
- Treat `li` / propriety as more than manners: as **practices that embody respect and calibrate conduct inside institutions**.

#### Concrete engine implications
- Good question pattern:
  - "What does this office owe, given the relationships already in force?"
  - "Which relationship is primary here, and what secondary duty still must be acknowledged?"
  - "What form of respectful correction or remonstration is owed before rupture or punishment?"
- Good concern pattern:
  - role-name mismatch
  - family / office collision
  - institutional disrespect disguised as efficiency
  - forced false harmony where real tension remains
- Good recommendation pattern:
  - preserve subordinated values where possible, not only the winning one
  - prefer correction / remonstration / role repair before naked force when domain fit allows

#### Cautions
- Do **not** turn this into blanket familial partiality.
- Do **not** let Confucian language weaken criminal-justice hazard detection.
- Do **not** infer that "harmony" means consensus; text supports tension-aware equilibrium, not sameness.

### 2) `care_ethics`

#### Adoptable now
- Strengthen the link between care and **concrete vulnerability plus dependence**, not abstract warmth.
- Import the idea that moral development and moral failure are strongly shaped by **conditions of nurture, security, and attention**.
- Use Mencian-style "extension" carefully: people often respond correctly nearby but fail to extend concern outward under pressure.

#### Concrete engine implications
- Better questions:
  - "Who is vulnerable here because institutions failed to provide the conditions required for decent agency?"
  - "Whose suffering is being cognitively acknowledged but not affectively extended?"
- Better concerns:
  - bureaucratic distance masking care failure
  - pressure or deprivation narrowing moral attention
  - formal legality without adequate responsiveness to dependence

#### Cautions
- Do not collapse `care_ethics` into generic compassion rhetoric.
- Do not use this text alone to create new branch families without case validation.

### 3) `institutional`

#### Adoptable now
- Responsibility can be **distributed upward** to institutions and rulers who create the conditions under which wrongdoing becomes likely.
- It is legitimate to ask whether governance structures are **entrapping** agents, then punishing downstream conduct they helped produce.
- Ritual / propriety can be operationalized as **stable procedures that preserve legitimacy, respect, and role discipline**.

#### Concrete engine implications
- Better concerns:
  - institution creates deprivation, panic, opacity, or incentive distortion, then blames direct actors only
  - legitimacy depends on repeatable respect-preserving procedure, not just outcome
- Better questions:
  - "What conditions did the institution create that predictably narrowed moral agency downstream?"
  - "What procedure would embody respect and legitimacy here, not merely technical compliance?"

#### Cautions
- Do not overgeneralize from king/ruler language into every org case without translation.
- Keep this as a legitimacy / conditions / distributed-responsibility lens, not a free-floating political theory layer.

### 4) `genealogical`

#### Adoptable now
- Very little directly.
- At most, the text supports a softer background caution that inherited norms and traditions are **contestable constructions**, not self-validating.

#### Speculative only
- Use Han/Mohist disagreement as support for asking whether a norm is justified by age, status, or actual function.

#### Not recommended now
- No direct rewrite of `genealogical` based on `han.txt`.
- The source is too broad and not targeted to the current live architecture.

## Classification

### Adoptable now
- Confucian = role-relational obligation, not generic benevolence
- Harmony = tension-aware equilibrium, not sameness or fake consensus
- Ritual/propriety = institutionally embodied respect practices
- Care = vulnerability plus dependence plus conditions for moral attention
- Institutional responsibility can extend to those who create the background conditions for failure
- Hard cases may require preserving acknowledgment of subordinated values rather than erasing them

### Speculative, needs case-backed validation
- More explicit remonstration / correction recommendation templates inside `confucian`
- Mencian "extension failure" as a recurring diagnostic pattern
- Institutional "entrapment" language outside criminal justice and governance-heavy cases
- Mohist/Confucian contrasts as a dedicated engine distinction

### Too abstract for direct engine use right now
- Neo-Confucian cosmology
- broad democracy / rights debates
- detailed Mencius vs Xunzi metaphysics of human nature
- aesthetic accounts of ritual except where they cash out into procedure / respect / legitimacy

## Recommended next implementation order

1. `confucian`
   - refine concern and question phrasing using role-relational / tension-aware language
2. `institutional`
   - add one or two distributed-responsibility formulations around background conditions
3. `care_ethics`
   - sharpen vulnerability / dependence / narrowed-attention language
4. leave `genealogical` alone for now

## Bottom line

Use `han.txt` as a **calibration source**, not a code authority. Its main value is to keep `confucian` from degenerating into generic kindness or fake harmony, and to help `institutional` and `care_ethics` speak more clearly about distributed responsibility, vulnerability, and legitimacy-preserving practice.
